Scrap Reduction

Location: Jatco BRP | Casting

Impact: -93% Scrap

➡️ Implemented quality and process improvements in Casting, achieving significant scrap reduction.

Production

Engineering

Industrial

Enterprise: Jatco Mexico

Location: Casting BRP in Site 1

Contest: QC-Story Contest 2025

Problem: In the casting area, a very high scrap rate was observed, mainly caused by internal porosity defects. To contain the issue, a temporary inspection process was implemented. However, this required additional workforce, which increased direct labor costs and impacted efficiency due to the extra time and resources consumed.

Solution: A series of process optimization trials were carried out in the casting equipment, adjusting key parameters of the injection and cooling stages to achieve uniform solidification and reduce internal defects.
After a trial period, the process proved stable, and a time study supported the removal of the temporary workforce, reducing costs and ensuring consistent product quality.

Impact:

My role: I coordinated the setup of the temporary inspection process, conducted time validation and efficiency analysis under abnormal conditions, and monitored production volumes to ensure compliance with targets. I also ensured product quality during the testing phase and led the final validation of the optimized process, supporting the workforce reduction and achieving both cost savings and efficiency improvements.

OOE Segregation by Model

Location: Jatco Nissan | Casting & Gears & Heat Treatments

Impact: +10% OOE (All Models)

➡️ Segregated workforce efficiency by model, comparing required hours vs. worked hours to optimize resource allocation and improve OOE.

Lean

Industrial

Enterprise: Jatco Mexico

Location: Jatco Site 1

Contest: QC-Story Contest 2025

Problem: At Jatco, Direct Labor Cost (DLC) was historically calculated by model, but workforce efficiency was always grouped as a single value. This meant that Model 1, Model 2, and Model 3 shared the same efficiency figure, even though their labor costs were different. As a result, monitor models were penalized, and the plant was losing money due to inaccurate efficiency allocation.

Solution: The project established a method to segregate workforce efficiency by model. Required hours were calculated for each model individually, providing a baseline of how much time should be consumed. In parallel, actual worked hours were obtained based on real production and proportionally distributed across total worked hours. This allowed the company to align cost per model with its actual workforce efficiency, eliminating distortions and ensuring a fair representation of labor usage.

Impact:

My role: I led the project end-to-end, validating and implementing the new method to segregate workforce efficiency by model. I conducted months of trials, developed impact and risk calculations, and designed the methodology to compare required vs. worked hours.

Judge Registration System

Location: Jatco BRP | Casting

Impact: +15% OOE Efficiency

➡️ Designed a standardized system for judge registration, improving traceability and reliability.

Quality

VBA

Industrial

Enterprise: Jatco Mexico

Location: Jatco Casting BRP Site 1

Contest: Intern Contest

Problem: In the Casting area for the BRP project, machine judgment records (OK/NG) were kept manually on paper. This created risks of operator errors when logging results, misplacing consecutive pieces, and ultimately losing traceability of the product.

I ensured the program’s usability for operators, its accuracy for traceability, and its reliability for long-term use in the casting process.

Automatic Direct Labor Cost Forecast

Location: Jatco Nissan | All Models

Impact: -750 hrs/man month

➡️ Developed a forecasting tool to simplify cost analysis and decision-making.

Industrial

VBA

Direct Labor Cost

Enterprise: Jatco Mexico

Location: Jatco Site 1 & Site 2

Contest: Intern Contest
